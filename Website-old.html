The most famous diamonds to exist! Diamonds have long been revered for their beauty and rarity. Throughout history, several diamonds have gained fame for their size, color, or storied histories. Here are some of the most famous diamonds in the world: Cullinan Diamond - The largest rough diamond ever found, discovered in South Africa in 1905. - It was cut into several large stones, including the Great Star of Africa (Cullinan I), which is set in the British Crown Jewels. Hope Diamond - A deep blue diamond weighing around 45.52 carats. - Known for its alleged curse, the diamond has been owned by various notable figures and is currently housed at the Smithsonian Institution in Washington, D.C. The Koh-i-Noor - A large, colorless diamond with a weight of around 105.6 carats. - It is part of the British Crown Jewels and has a long and complex history involving various royal dynasties in India and beyond. The Regent Diamond - A cushion-cut diamond weighing approximately 140.64 carats. - Once owned by Louis XV of France, it is now on display at the Louvre Museum in Paris. The Orlov Diamond - A large rose-cut diamond weighing approximately 189.62 carats. - Named after Count Orlov, who is said to have purchased the diamond and gifted it to Empress Catherine the Great of Russia. It is now part of the Kremlin Diamond Fund. The Sancy Diamond - A pale yellow diamond weighing around 55.23 carats. - Known for its shield shape, the diamond has a storied history involving various European royal families. The Wittelsbach-Graff Diamond - A deep blue diamond weighing around 31.06 carats. - Originally part of the Bavarian Crown Jewels, the diamond was recut by Laurence Graff in 2008. The Darya-i-Noor - A pale pink diamond weighing around 182 carats. - It is one of the largest diamonds in the world and is part of the Iranian Crown Jewels. These diamonds are renowned not only for their size and beauty but also for their historical significance and associations with royalty and nobility. Many of them are displayed in museums and form part of the world's most valuable collections of gemstones.
